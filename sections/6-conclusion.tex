\chapter{Conclusion}
\label{chap:conclusion}

This thesis studied what it takes to scale a scene-level 3D Gaussian
Splatting variational autoencoder from a few hundred scenes to several
thousand, and whether the resulting latent can carry semantic structure.

\paragraph{RQ1 (Ordering and identifiability).}
Yes, canonical ordering is essential. Without a spatially consistent serial
order, the slot-aligned reconstruction target changes identity from scene to
scene and the model cannot converge. A fixed-frame Hilbert serialisation
provides locality---consecutive slots are always spatially adjacent---making
the slot-to-position function learnable and consistent across the training
set. This is the most impactful single change we made.

\paragraph{RQ2 (Architecture and generalisation).}
Yes, the local encoder is the key architectural change. The globally-pooled
bottleneck encoder produces latent tokens that are functions of the entire
scene, which leads to memorisation ($5$--$8\times$ train/held-out gap) at
scale. The windowed local encoder produces compositional tokens: similar
local patches activate similar features regardless of which scene they come
from, closing the generalisation gap to $\approx 1\times$.

\paragraph{RQ3 (Semantic structure).}
Yes, at 300 scenes, per-primitive contrastive supervision and latent
disentanglement produce meaningful semantic structure without degrading
reconstruction. The cross-reconstruction test reduces geometry disruption
from a cross-scene $z_s$ swap by $80\times$; the InfoNCE features achieve
$\approx\!26\%$ linear-probe accuracy over 72 categories. The Strategy-B1
layout code is especially stable under partial observation (0.761 cosine
similarity at 40\% coverage), which is the right property for the scene
completion pipeline.

\paragraph{Main takeaway.}
Scaling a 3DGS autoencoder to thousands of frozen scenes requires solving
two independent problems in sequence: (1) making the reconstruction target
identifiable by ordering and (2) making the encoder compositional by
localisation. These are not minor hyperparameter changes---they fundamentally
alter what the model can learn. Any future work deploying a 3DGS VAE on
pre-computed scene reconstructions should account for the frozen-splat regime,
where optimal sampling budgets differ significantly from the joint-optimisation
setting.

\paragraph{Looking ahead.}
With the Stage-1 ablation converging, the immediate next step is training and
evaluating the Stage-2 hierarchical generator for unconditional scene
generation and partial-scan completion. These experiments, together with
image-space quality metrics on the final Stage-1 checkpoint, will provide the
full quantitative picture needed for a conference-quality research paper.
