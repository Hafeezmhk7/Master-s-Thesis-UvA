\chapter{Related Work}
\label{chap:related}

This chapter reviews four bodies of work that directly bear on what we do:
explicit 3D scene representations, generative models for 3D content, learning
on unordered point sets, and semantically structured representations. Each
section closes by identifying the gap that motivates our contribution.

\section{3D Scene Representations}
\label{sec:rw_repr}

\paragraph{Neural radiance fields.}
Mildenhall et al.~\citeyearpar{mildenhall2020nerf} proposed NeRF, a continuous
volumetric scene representation encoded in the weights of a coordinate-conditioned
MLP. NeRF produces photorealistic renders and became the dominant approach for
novel-view synthesis. However, rendering is slow (requiring hundreds of
network evaluations per ray), and the per-scene weight representation is
difficult to use generatively---there is no explicit parameter to predict.

\paragraph{3D Gaussian Splatting.}
Kerbl et al.~\citeyearpar{kerbl2023gaussian} proposed 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS),
representing a scene as a set of anisotropic Gaussian primitives rasterised
by alpha-compositing. The approach achieves real-time rendering at comparable
quality to NeRF, and the explicit structure opens a path towards direct
network-based generation. Each primitive carries:
\begin{itemize}
  \item a position $\vmu \in \bR^3$,
  \item a colour $c \in \bR^3$ (DC spherical-harmonic coefficient),
  \item an opacity $\alpha \in (0,1)$,
  \item a scale $s \in \bR^3_+$ and a unit quaternion $q \in \bS^3$ that
        together define the anisotropic covariance
        $\vsigma = R(q)\,\mathrm{diag}(s^2)\,R(q)^\top$.
\end{itemize}
Scaffold-GS~\citep{lu2024scaffold} reduces the structural redundancy of a
dense 3DGS fit by anchoring Gaussian attributes to a sparse neural scaffold,
predicting local displacements rather than absolute parameters. We borrow
the anchor-plus-local-offset idea in our decoder
(\S\ref{sec:method_anchor}).

\paragraph{Gap.} Despite the explicitness of 3DGS, scene-level \emph{generative}
modelling requires a compact organised latent, which neither 3DGS itself nor
its extensions directly provide.

\section{Latent Generative Modelling of 3D Content}
\label{sec:rw_gen}

\paragraph{Two-stage latent generation.}
The standard two-stage recipe~\citep{rombach2022ldm} trains a VAE to compress
the signal, then trains a generative model---originally DDPM~\citep{ho2020ddpm},
now more commonly flow matching~\citep{lipman2023flowmatching,liu2023rectified}
with a transformer backbone~\citep{peebles2023dit,ma2024sit}---in the
latent space. This recipe decouples the complexity of the raw representation
from the generative learning problem.

\paragraph{Object-level 3D generation.}
LION~\citep{zeng2022lion} applies hierarchical latent diffusion to point cloud
objects. Michelangelo~\citep{zhao2023michelangelo} uses a cross-attention
Perceiver~\citep{jaegle2021perceiver,jaegle2022perceiverio} to encode a 3D
shape into a fixed token set aligned with image and text embeddings;
its shape-latent VAE is the architectural ancestor of ours.
GaussianCube~\citep{zhang2024gaussiancube} fits a fixed-count object-level
Gaussian set, transports it to a regular voxel grid via optimal transport
so a standard convolutional diffusion model can operate on it. Its
grid-structuring insight---position as a structural reference plus a small
offset---is a close analogue of our anchor-relative decoder, though the
dense grid assumption does not transfer to unbounded scenes.

\paragraph{Scene-level 3DGS.}
Can3Tok~\citep{gao2025can3tok} is the direct predecessor of this work. It
encodes a full 3DGS scene with a Perceiver cross-attention bottleneck and
decodes the Gaussian attributes with a transformer and flat MLP. Can3Tok
demonstrated convergence on datasets of a few hundred scenes per training run
and showed conditional generation as an application. SceneSplat~\citep{li2025scenesplat}
introduced the first generalised feed-forward scene encoder, pre-trained on
SceneSplat-7K---7,916 indoor 3DGS scenes with per-Gaussian ScanNet-72
category labels---for open-vocabulary scene understanding. We consume
SceneSplat-7K's per-Gaussian labels for our semantic experiments.

\paragraph{Gap.} What is required to scale a scene-level 3DGS VAE from hundreds
to thousands of scenes, and why naive scaling fails, has not been studied.

\section{Learning on Unordered Sets and Space-Filling Curves}
\label{sec:rw_sets}

\paragraph{Permutation-invariant encoders.}
PointNet~\citep{qi2017pointnet} established symmetric aggregation for point
clouds. The Perceiver~\citep{jaegle2021perceiver} generalises this through
cross-attention to a fixed set of learned queries. These give permutation
invariance on the encoder side at no cost. The problem reappears at the
decoder: any loss comparing predicted slot $i$ to target slot $i$ depends on
an arbitrary ordering.

\paragraph{Canonical ordering via space-filling curves.}
A complementary approach fixes a canonical order rather than handling
permutation invariance in the loss. Point Transformer V3~\citep{wu2024ptv3}
serialises point cloud tokens along the Hilbert space-filling
curve~\citep{skilling2004hilbert} for windowed attention, finding that the
Hilbert curve's superior locality---consecutive indices are always spatially
adjacent, unlike the Z-order (Morton) curve where a high-order bit flip
causes large spatial jumps---gives better downstream performance. This
locality property is exactly what we need for a slot-to-position
reconstruction target to be learnable.

\paragraph{Gap.} Canonical serialisation is established for point cloud
processing but has not been applied to the 3DGS reconstruction target
in a way that interacts with a fixed-frame positional decoder.

\section{Semantic and Disentangled Representations}
\label{sec:rw_sem}

\paragraph{Contrastive learning.}
Self-supervised contrastive learning~\citep{oord2018cpc,chen2020simclr} has
demonstrated that pulling together similar instances and pushing apart
dissimilar ones in embedding space produces rich, transferable features.
The InfoNCE objective~\citep{oord2018cpc} is the standard tool; we adapt it
to the per-Gaussian level for our semantic experiments, using per-Gaussian
ScanNet-72 labels from SceneSplat-7K as the positive-pair criterion.

\paragraph{Disentangled latent representations.}
The $\beta$-VAE~\citep{higgins2017betavae} and its successors attempt to
separate independent factors of variation in the latent space. Locatello
et al.~\citeyearpar{locatello2019challenging} showed that fully unsupervised
disentanglement is unidentifiable without inductive bias, motivating our
use of weak structural supervision (auxiliary heads and cross-reconstruction
losses) rather than unsupervised disentanglement.

\paragraph{Gap.} Dense per-Gaussian semantic labels for 3DGS scenes are now
available via SceneSplat-7K~\citep{li2025scenesplat}, but have only been
used for discriminative scene understanding, not to add semantic structure
to a generative latent.
